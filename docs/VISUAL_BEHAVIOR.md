# Visual Behavior Target

| Condition    | Collision read               | Floc read                    | Clearing front           | Fixed-endpoint appearance    |
| ------------ | ---------------------------- | ---------------------------- | ------------------------ | ---------------------------- |
| Underdose    | Few successful collisions    | Fine particles and microfloc | Minimal or diffuse       | Persistent uniform haze      |
| Near optimum | Rapid successful aggregation | Large readable floc          | Strong top-down clearing | Clearly improved upper water |
| Overdose     | Reduced sustained sticking   | Weak or transient clusters   | Limited or late          | Residual haze above optimum  |

Compressed target ranges are 4-8 seconds rapid mix, 10-20 seconds flocculation, 12-25 seconds settling, and 1-3 seconds measurement. These are perceptual starting ranges, not physical-time claims.

The model should preserve the causal sequence while exaggerating representative particle size, visibility, and timing enough to read at arm's length. Overdose does not need a theatrical visual signature when the physical dose position and accumulated U-curve complete the interpretation. Renderer-only effects must never contradict the authoritative turbidity bands.

The hero observation tank is the only live process visualization and must remain visually dominant. The six-jar rack anchors recognition and shows static canonical summaries for doses 0, 2, 4, 6, 8, and 10. The mounted plot, not the jars, is the complete visual memory for every dose from 0 through 10.

Operator-informed and non-operator review notes will be added during desktop and headset tuning.
