# Reference Library Sources

Reviewed: 2026-07-20

## Inclusion policy

The in-app pages are original educational summaries written for Sunol FlowLab
VR. The application links to public source documents; it does not reproduce
their pages, figures, trademarks, or videos. Public links are supporting
references, not endorsements. The in-headset `WEB` button opens an official
HTML resource page rather than a direct PDF because Quest Browser may pause PDF
downloads instead of displaying them. It uses the current browser tab because
Quest blocks popup tabs triggered from an immersive controller event. Opening
`WEB` therefore exits VR; Browser Back returns to FlowLab, where the visitor can
enter VR again. The exact documents remain linked below.

AWWA material may be cited or linked when publicly available. Subscription
training media is not embedded, downloaded, or represented as part of this
project without explicit permission.

## Book sources

### Coagulation & Flocculation

Primary source:

- California State Water Resources Control Board,
  [Safe Drinking Water Plan for California](https://www.waterboards.ca.gov/publications_forms/publications/legislative/docs/2015/sdwp.pdf).

Headset-safe landing page:

- [Safe Drinking Water Plan](https://www.waterboards.ca.gov/drinking_water/safedrinkingwaterplan/).

Use in the app: an original three-page explanation of colloidal particles,
coagulation, flocculation, later physical removal, and the importance of source
water and treatment conditions.

### How Jar Testing Works

Primary source:

- California State Water Resources Control Board, Division of Drinking Water,
  [Jar Testing Made Easy](https://water.waterboards.ca.gov/drinking_water/programs/districts/docs/mendocino/jar_testing_made_easy_aug7_2020.pdf).

Headset-safe landing page:

- [Mendocino District Jar Testing Resources](https://www.waterboards.ca.gov/drinking_water/programs/districts/mendocino_district.html).

Use in the app: an original explanation of side-by-side controlled comparison,
representative mixing and settling, consistent chemical addition, observation,
measurement, and filterability.

### Interpreting Jar-Test Results

Primary source:

- California State Water Resources Control Board, Division of Drinking Water,
  [Jar Testing Made Easy](https://water.waterboards.ca.gov/drinking_water/programs/districts/docs/mendocino/jar_testing_made_easy_aug7_2020.pdf).

Headset-safe landing page:

- [Mendocino District Jar Testing Resources](https://www.waterboards.ca.gov/drinking_water/programs/districts/mendocino_district.html).

Use in the app: an original explanation of useful treatment ranges,
underdose/overdose comparison, measured outcomes, and cautious transfer to
full-scale treatment.

### Enhanced Coagulation

Primary source:

- U.S. Environmental Protection Agency,
  [Enhanced Coagulation and Enhanced Precipitative Softening Guidance Manual](https://nepis.epa.gov/Exe/ZyPDF.cgi/200021WV.PDF?Dockey=200021WV.PDF),
  EPA 815-R-99-012.

Headset-safe landing page:

- [EPA Guidance Manuals for the Surface Water Treatment Rules](https://www.epa.gov/dwreginfo/guidance-manuals-surface-water-treatment-rules).

Use in the app: an original overview of natural organic matter and TOC removal,
disinfection-byproduct precursors, the influence of pH and alkalinity, and the
Step 2 point-of-diminishing-return concept. The page explicitly directs real
compliance decisions to current rules, state approval, and site-specific
professional evaluation.
